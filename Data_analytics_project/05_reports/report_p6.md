# Practical 6: Power BI Executive Dashboard and Insight Story

## Objective
The objective of this practical is to design a single-page interactive dashboard for executives. The dashboard presents important business KPIs and visual insights that help management make informed decisions quickly.

## Dashboard Components

### KPI Cards
- Total Sales
- Profit Margin
- Average Shipping Delay
- Order Count (if added)

### Visuals
1. Sales by Country (Map)
2. High Sales vs High Profit by Sub-Category (Scatter Chart)
3. Sales Trend Over Time (Line Chart)
4. Sales by Category (Clustered Column Chart)

### Slicers
- Order Date
- Category
- Region

---

# Report Questions

## 1. What is the dashboard's main business message?

The dashboard provides a clear overview of business performance by displaying total sales, profit margin, shipping performance, and sales trends. It helps management identify profitable categories, high-performing regions, and business growth over time.

---

## 2. Which filter changes the story most strongly?

The **Region** filter changes the dashboard most strongly because it affects sales, profit, shipping delay, and category performance across different geographical areas.

---

## 3. What would you improve if you had one more day?

If I had one more day, I would:
- Add drill-through pages for detailed analysis.
- Add customer and product-level insights.
- Improve the dashboard design with custom icons and better formatting.
- Add more KPI indicators and conditional formatting.
- Optimize the dashboard for faster performance.

---

# Conclusion

The executive dashboard successfully summarizes key business information in a single page. It enables decision-makers to monitor sales, profit, shipping performance, regional performance, and category trends through interactive visuals and filters.
